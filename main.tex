\section{Two Nambu-Goto Strings}

Following the effective-string discussion of Aharony and Komargodski,
\href{https://arxiv.org/abs/1302.6257}{arXiv:1302.6257}, the leading
weight-zero term for a long string is the Nambu-Goto action, namely the
string tension times the induced worldsheet area.  For two independent
strings, let
\begin{equation}
  X_{(s)}^{\mu} = X_{(s)}^{\mu}(\sigma_s^0,\sigma_s^1),
  \qquad s=1,2,
\end{equation}
be the two spacetime embeddings.  The induced metric on the worldsheet of
string $s$ is
\begin{equation}
  h^{(s)}_{ab}
  = \partial_a X_{(s)}^{\mu}\,\partial_b X^{(s)}_{\mu},
  \qquad a,b=0,1 .
\end{equation}
The Nambu-Goto action for two decoupled strings is therefore the sum of the
two induced areas,
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{NG}}^{(1+2)}
  =
  -\sum_{s=1}^{2} T_s \int d^2\sigma_s\,
  \sqrt{-\det h^{(s)}_{ab}} .
  \label{eq:two-string-ng-covariant}
\end{equation}
Here $T_s$ is the tension of string $s$; the equal-tension case follows by
setting $T_1=T_2=T$.

In static gauge, with $X_{(s)}^0=\sigma_s^0\equiv \tau_s$ and
$X_{(s)}^1=\sigma_s^1\equiv \sigma_s$, the transverse fluctuations are
$X_{(s)}^i(\tau_s,\sigma_s)$ for $i=2,\ldots,D-1$.  Using the mostly-plus
worldsheet metric $\eta_{ab}=\operatorname{diag}(-1,+1)$, the same action
may be written as
\begin{equation}
  S_{\mathrm{NG}}^{(1+2)}
  =
  -\sum_{s=1}^{2} T_s \int d^2\sigma_s\,
  \sqrt{-\det\!\left(
    \eta_{ab}
    + \partial_a X_{(s)}^i\,\partial_b X_{(s)}^i
  \right)} .
  \label{eq:two-string-ng-static-gauge}
\end{equation}
Equivalently, the static-gauge Lagrangian density for string $s$ is
\begin{align}
  \mathcal{L}_{s}
  =
  -T_s
  \sqrt{
    \left(1-\dot{X}_{(s)}^i\dot{X}_{(s)}^i\right)
    \left(1+X_{(s)}^{j\prime}X_{(s)}^{j\prime}\right)
    +
    \left(\dot{X}_{(s)}^i X_{(s)}^{i\prime}\right)^2
  } .
  \label{eq:single-string-ng-density}
\end{align}
Dots and primes denote derivatives with respect to $\tau_s$ and $\sigma_s$,
respectively, and repeated transverse indices are summed.  If the two
worldsheets are described using common coordinates, the total Lagrangian
density is simply
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{tot}} = \mathcal{L}_1 + \mathcal{L}_2 .
\end{equation}
No interaction term has been included; equations
\eqref{eq:two-string-ng-covariant}--\eqref{eq:single-string-ng-density}
represent two independent Nambu-Goto strings.
